    2.1:Current Situation - How do the Education Bureau take action

    Announcement

    Taking into account the latest epidemic development, the Education Bureau will make announcements on class suspension and resumption arrangements for all schools (including kindergartens, primary schools, secondary schools, special schools, and private schools offering non-formal curricula). Higher education institutions will also make announcements on their program arrangements.

    Activities

    An EDB spokesman stated, "The EDB places significant attention to students' health and periodically reviews various anti-epidemic measures in schools. We will relax the requirements for students to wear masks during half-day activities like speech days, parents' days, open days, and campus visits in response to the government's earlier announcement that the second stage of the relaxation in social distance measures will be implemented today as scheduled. Students who attend the activities must still adhere to the venue's pertinent anti-epidemic prevention requirements.

    Furthermore, secondary schools, elementary schools, and kindergartens can set up Saturday half-day participation in non-academic mask-wearing extracurricular activities (such as music or sports events, and school team practice).

    2.3:Guildlines for school

    The EDB has updated the guidelines on "Health Protection Measures for Schools" and uploaded them onto the EDB's website.

    Guildlines for school:https://www.chp.gov.hk/files/pdf/advice_to_school_on_prevention_of_nid_eng.pdf

    Preventive Measure provided in guildlines
  • A.General Advice

    • example:School should post up health education materials on hand hygiene, cough etiquette and COVID-19 in conspicuous sites to alert staff and students. Relevant health education materials can be downloaded from the Centre for Health Protection (CHP) website

  • B.COVID-19 Testing

    • example:If a student is a close contact or household member of a confirmed or preliminary confirmed case, the student must not attend school and should undergo quarantine and / or testing as instructed by the CHP.

  • C. Temperature Screening

    • example:School should check temperature for students, staff and visitors upon entry to the school premises.

  • D. Hand Hygiene Facilities

    • example: Provide liquid soap and disposable paper towels at places where there are handwashing facilities, e.g. toilets, kitchens, tuck shops / canteens, art rooms, activity rooms as indicated

  • E. Maintain Good Indoor Ventilation

    • Open windows as appropriate for fresh air.

  • F. Water Dispenser

    • example:Do not use water dispensers to wash hands / personal items.

  • G. Maintain Environment Clean and Hygienic

    • example:Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces, furniture, toys, commonly shared items and floor frequently.
